Bikeshare and Equity in Philadelphia

How well does Philly's Indego serve its diverse population?


As bikeshare becomes more popular across the U.S., cities must take steps to ensure that all residents have equal access, regardless of factors such as race and socioeconomic status.


This interactive map shows how the location of current Indego bikeshare stations in Philadelphia relates to poverty and race. Toggle the layers, or click on a neighborhood to see its statistics.


Race and poverty data were calculated from 2015 American Community Survey 5-year Estimates. Indego kiosks were mapped from opendataphilly.org, and the half mile walking buffer to stations was calculated in Esri ArcGIS using street data from opendataphilly.org. Neighborhood boundaries are based on definitions by Azavea, Inc., but were adjusted to be coterminous with census tract boundaries.
